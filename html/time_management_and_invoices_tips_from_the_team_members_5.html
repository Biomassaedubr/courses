I've tried to use an external application like others once, and I quickly felt that the process of copying over work logs all the time wasn't for me.

So instead, for large tasks, I often take a look at the current time through the phone or clock, and come back to it later when I feel like my task is done, and track the difference. For smaller tasks, I usually have a good idea of how long it'll take (i.e. 3-5 minutes), and don't bother with the overhead of a timer in these cases.

A funny reminder for these timers: I've awkwardly forgotten in the past to log my time for the task while running the timer, and when I come back the next day there's 24+ hours of time ticking along. Don't forget to track it!

As for general time management, I don't think this technique will be useful for most people, but for me: I essentially make a mental or written down plan of how I'll execute my tasks for the week at the beginning. I then tackle each task at appropriate times depending on how much I believe each should take based off of my planning. This used to be impossible without having context with the task's content, but over time it's becoming pretty clear to me the path these things generally take.
