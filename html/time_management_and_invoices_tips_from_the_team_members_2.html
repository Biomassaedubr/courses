I use Project Hamster , which I find has a very quick, easy, reliable interface. It's a GTK project, but I believe it's tightly integrated with other components in the freedesktop/GNOME ecosystem, so it would probably be difficult if not impossible to make it work outside of Linux or FreeBSD.

Either way, I imagine there are plenty of tools that do just as good a job for Windows or macOS.

My usual pattern is this:

  • For keeping track of larger tasks during the day (things I think will take > 10-15 minutes), I track these directly with Hamster. I actually copy and paste the full name of the OpenCraft ticket into Hamster when I start logging; this makes logging the time on JIRA much easier later on.
  • I try to remain at Inbox Zero as much as possible, so I respond to most small things immediately, prioritize my email as a task list for stuff I choose not to respond to immediately (usually when they're bigger notifications), and clear out the bigger notifications in between tasks (for this, it helps to use Google Inbox instead of Gmail, although there are some downsides to it and the OpenCraft org itself doesn't have Inbox enabled).
  • For the smaller email notifications (usually < 10 minutes), I log my time directly on the corresponding JIRA tickets right away using the 'W' hotkey and tabbing through the fields. It takes no time at all to enter worklogs this way, and there's no point in tossing this stuff into Hamster since they're so small.
  • At the end of my day, I go to the Worklog Calendar view In JIRA and record all the time I have recorded in OpenCraft.
